Given

• P₀ = ₱100,000 invested at i = 25% per year
• At Year 3: Borrow ₱50,000 from bank at 20% annual
• At Year 5: Withdraw ALL from fund to repay bank loan + interest
• From Year 6 onward: Take ₱20,000/year out of remaining fund
• Find: How much total was withdrawn at Year 5?

Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1 — Fund Value at Year 5

Grow ₱100,000 for 5 years at 25%:

F = P(1 + i)ⁿ
n = 5, i = 0.25
F = 100,000 × (1.25)⁵
(1.25)⁵ = 3.05176
F = 100,000 × 3.05176 = ₱305,176
Step 2 — Loan Amount Owed at Year 5

The ₱50,000 borrowed at Year 3 grows for 2 years at 20%:

n = 2, i = 0.20
Loan due = 50,000 × (1.20)²
(1.20)² = 1.44
Loan due = 50,000 × 1.44 = ₱72,000
Step 3 — Amount Withdrawn at Year 5

All the fund is used to repay the loan. The withdrawal = what's left after repaying:

Withdrawn = Fund Value − Loan Due
Withdrawn = 305,176 − 72,000 = ₱233,176
✅ Amount Withdrawn at Year 5 = ₱233,176
The remaining ₱233,176 is then the present worth of the ₱20,000/year annuity going forward from Year 6.

Given

• Deposit A per year, Years 1–12 (n = 12 deposits)
• i = 8% per year
• Withdraw ₱309/year, Years 15–19 (n = 5 withdrawals)
• Gap between last deposit (Yr 12) and first withdrawal (Yr 15) = 2 years idle
• Find: Annual deposit A

Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1 — PW of Withdrawals at Year 14

The 5 withdrawals of ₱309 start at Year 15 → their PW is at Year 14:

P = A × [(1+i)ⁿ - 1] / [i(1+i)ⁿ]
A = 309, i = 0.08, n = 5
(1.08)⁵ = 1.46933
P₁₄ = 309 × [(1.46933 - 1) / (0.08 × 1.46933)]
P₁₄ = 309 × [0.46933 / 0.117546]
P₁₄ = 309 × 3.99271 = ₱1,233.75
Step 2 — Bring P₁₄ Back to Year 0

Discount ₱1,233.75 back 14 years to Year 0:

P₀ = P₁₄ / (1.08)¹⁴
(1.08)¹⁴ = 2.93719
P₀ = 1,233.75 / 2.93719 = ₱420.07
Step 3 — Find Annual Deposit A

This P₀ = ₱420.07 must equal the PW of 12 annual deposits:

P₀ = A × [(1.08)¹² - 1] / [0.08 × (1.08)¹²]
(1.08)¹² = 2.51817
420.07 = A × [1.51817 / (0.08 × 2.51817)]
420.07 = A × [1.51817 / 0.20145]
420.07 = A × 7.5361
A = 420.07 / 7.5361
✅ Annual Deposit A = ₱55.74 per year
This is a deferred-style ordinary annuity. The key is to find the PW of all withdrawals first, bring it back to Year 0, then solve for the equal deposit A.

F = A × [(1+i)ⁿ - 1] / i

8,000,000 = A × 18.4185
A = 8,000,000 / 18.4185
✅ Annual Deposit A = ₱434,359.37 per year
Deposits at END of each year = Ordinary Annuity. The fund grows with 13% interest each year and the accumulated deposits reach ₱8,000,000 by Year 10.

Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1 — Grow ₱50,000 for 14 Years

The money grows until Year 14, which is the PW point of the 6-payment annuity:

F₁₄ = 50,000 × (1.14)¹⁴

(1.14)⁴ = 1.6890
(1.14)⁷ = 2.5023

F₁₄ = 50,000 × 6.2615 = ₱313,075
Step 2 — ₱313,075 = PW of 6 Annual Payments
PW = A × [(1+i)ⁿ - 1] / [i(1+i)ⁿ]
313,075 = A × [(1.14)⁶ - 1] / [0.14 × (1.14)⁶]

(1.14)⁶ = 2.1950
Factor = (2.1950 - 1) / (0.14 × 2.1950)
= 1.1950 / 0.30730
= 3.8887

313,075 = A × 3.8887
A = 313,075 / 3.8887
✅ Annual Withdrawal A = ₱80,508 per year (Ages 17–22)
The key in deferred annuity: first find how much the investment grows during the waiting period (14 years), then use that as the present worth to find A.

CC₂ = 100,000 / [(1.15)¹⁰ - 1]
(1.15)¹⁰ = 4.0456
CC₂ = 100,000 / (4.0456 - 1)

P₄ = 100,000 × [(1.15)⁴ - 1] / [0.15 × (1.15)⁴]
(1.15)⁴ = 1.7490
P₄ = 100,000 × [0.7490 / (0.15 × 1.7490)]
P₄ = 100,000 × [0.7490 / 0.26235]
P₄ = 100,000 × 2.8550 = ₱285,498
Component 3B — Operating Cost ₱160,000/yr forever (from Year 5 onward)

Perpetuity of ₱160,000/yr starting Year 5 → PW at Year 4, then discount to Year 0:

PW at Year 4 = 160,000 / 0.15 = ₱1,066,667
PW at Year 0 = 1,066,667 / (1.15)⁴
= 1,066,667 / 1.7490 = ₱610,101

CC₃ = 285,498 + 610,101 = ₱895,599
Component 4 — ₱300,000 every 13 years
CC₄ = 300,000 / [(1.15)¹³ - 1]
(1.15)¹³ = 6.1528
CC₄ = 300,000 / (6.1528 - 1)
CC₄ = 300,000 / 5.1528 = ₱58,222
Total Capitalized Cost
CC = CC₁ + CC₂ + CC₃ + CC₄
CC = 3,000,000 + 32,834 + 895,599 + 58,222
✅ Total Capitalized Cost = ₱3,986,655

Cash Price = Down Payment + PW of Installments
Cash Price = 3,000 + 5,453.77
✅ Equivalent Cash Price = ₱8,453.77
Cash price means: what would you pay TODAY if you paid in full instead of installments? It equals the down payment plus the present value of all future payments.
